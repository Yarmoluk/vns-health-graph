The 10 Reports — What Each Found

Bessemer Venture Partners — State of Health AI 2026

AI captured 55% of all healthtech funding in 2025, up from 37% in 2024 and 29% in 2022. 527 venture deals totaling ~$14B. Average round size up 42% to $29M. Series D+ valuations jumped 63%.

Mega-rounds: Abridge $300M at $5B, Ambiance $243M at $1.04B, Function Health $300M at $2.2B, rumored OpenEvidence $250M at $11.75B.

2026 prediction: Breakout year driven by clinical AI adoption, AI-first value-based care companies, and a new category of data infrastructure tools.

bvp.com/atlas/state-of-health-ai-2026

NVIDIA — Healthcare AI ROI Survey 2026

600+ healthcare professionals surveyed. 70% actively using AI (up from 63%). 69% using GenAI/LLMs (up from 54%). 85% of management report AI increased revenue. 80% cite reduced costs.

44% say AI increased revenue by more than 10%. Small companies benefit most: 56% report >10% revenue growth from AI.

For payers/providers specifically: 39% cite admin tasks and workflow optimization as top ROI area. 85% expect AI budgets to increase in 2026.

NVIDIA Healthcare AI Survey 2026

Deloitte — State of AI in Enterprise + Healthcare Outlook 2026

80%+ of health execs expect both agentic AI and gen-AI to deliver moderate-to-significant value in 2026. But: 49% are still experimenting. 18% haven't adopted AI at all. Only ~33% are using AI at scale.

75% of healthcare leaders are scaling generative AI. 80%+ of health systems prioritize agentic AI for clinical ops and revenue cycle management.

The gap: The question isn't whether to adopt — it's whether to stay in pilots or invest in enterprise scaling that reshapes workflows.

Deloitte State of AI 2026

Wolters Kluwer — The Shadow AI Crisis

Nearly 20% of healthcare staff admit to using unauthorized AI tools. 40% have encountered them. 50% cite "faster workflows" as their primary motivation.

The governance gap: healthcare data breaches average $7.4M. Unvetted clinical AI advice creates patient safety risk and massive liability.

The insight: Shadow AI is a symptom of enterprise AI tools being too slow, too locked down, or non-existent. Staff are solving their own problems. The question is whether organizations will catch up with governance before an incident forces it.

McKinsey — State of AI + Healthcare 2026

92% of executives plan to increase AI investment over the next 3 years. Nearly 80% of organizations use AI in at least one function.

In healthcare specifically: technology has evolved beyond a supporting function to become the primary driver of growth. AI and data are redefining efficiency across the US healthcare system.

BCG — AI Agents Transforming Healthcare + Pharma

BCG partnered with Hippocratic AI (Jan 2026) to deploy agentic AI across biopharma and medtech. Most pharma companies are running AI pilots; few are scaling.

The real promise per BCG: "AI can uncover new biology. It can design new drugs that humans otherwise couldn't do or will take forever to do." But the immediate ROI is in operations and workflows, not drug discovery.

Over 60% of 228 AI-first healthcare companies are US/Canada-based.

The Convergence — Six Themes Across All 10 Reports

1. The Adoption-Scale Gap Is the Real Story

70% are "using AI" (NVIDIA). But only 33% at scale (Deloitte). Only 2% enterprise-wide. 49% still experimenting. The industry has adopted AI in pilots but cannot operationalize it. The bottleneck isn't technology — it's data infrastructure, governance, and workflow integration.

2. Money Is Flowing — Into Infrastructure, Not Just Models

$14B in venture funding, 55% of all healthtech (Bessemer). 92% planning to increase investment (McKinsey). 85% expect budget increases (NVIDIA). But Bessemer specifically calls out "data infrastructure tools" as the emerging category for 2026. The market is realizing that AI models without connected data foundations underperform.

3. Shadow AI Is the Governance Crisis Nobody Planned For

20% of staff using unauthorized AI. 40% encountering it (Wolters Kluwer). $7.4M average breach cost. This is what happens when enterprise AI tools lag behind individual need. The organizations that solve this fastest will be the ones who provide governed, connected AI tools that are actually faster than the shadow alternatives.

4. Explainability Is No Longer Optional

Investors want "glass box" AI (Business Insider). CMS-0057-F requires explainable prior auth decisions. Kiran Kumar Prabu (in Effie's comments) notes the shift from "human-in-the-loop" to "human-over-the-loop" as 2026's biggest regulatory challenge. Knowledge graphs are inherently explainable — every answer has a traversable reasoning path.

5. Payer Operations Is the Highest-ROI Application Layer

39% of payers/providers cite admin tasks as top AI ROI (NVIDIA). EY confirms AI is streamlining claims adjudication. Deloitte shows 80%+ prioritizing revenue cycle management. The back office — claims, auth, quality, compliance — is where AI pays for itself fastest in healthcare. This is exactly where connected data (provider directories, claim lifecycles, member journeys) creates the most value.

6. The Structural Problem Is Data Connection, Not Data Collection

Every report implicitly acknowledges this: healthcare organizations have massive amounts of data. They cannot connect it across systems, vendors, and workflows. Pilots succeed in isolation because the data is controlled. Enterprise scale fails because the data is fragmented. This is the knowledge graph thesis: the value is not in the data you have, but in the relationships between data you can't currently see.
