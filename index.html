Problem #1
"When did you become aware?"

What CMS actually asks isn't "Is Dr. Smith's address correct?" It's: "When did you learn it was wrong, what did you do, and how fast?"

Salesforce shows the record was updated March 3. It cannot prove whether VNS knew on January 20 (from a member call), February 5 (from credentialing), or March 1 (from 90-day outreach).

Provider moves offices Jan 15
→ Maybe tells VNS. Maybe doesn't.
→ Salesforce updated March 3
→ CMS: "When did you know?"
→ Can't prove awareness timeline
→ Corrective Action Plan
→ Directory SUPPRESSED on Plan Finder
→ Enrollment stalls — all plans

What You Can Do

Build a change audit layer on your provider records. Every update gets a timestamp, source channel (member call, credentialing, outreach), who detected it, what action was taken, and resolution time.

This isn't a new system. It's metadata on your existing Salesforce provider objects — a Field History Tracking implementation with structured source attribution.

Result: CMS asks "when?" — you pull the audit trail. Awareness: Jan 20, 2:32 PM. Response: 38 minutes. SLA: met.

Why This Is Urgent

VNS is expanding into 25 new counties. Each one adds hundreds of provider records you've never managed. Without provenance tracking, every new county = new compliance exposure.

A provider cannot see: "I submitted auth Monday, approved Wednesday, billed Thursday, now in Varis review."

That view doesn't exist anywhere. Not for providers. Not for VNS internally. Four separate data silos, four logins, four status vocabularies.

Plus: cross-year billing rule (2025/2026 services on separate claims) is generating predictable, preventable denials right now in Q1 2026.

What You Can Do

Unify the claim lifecycle in Salesforce. Pull auth status from eviCore, claim status from Availity, and audit flags from Varis into a single timeline view on the provider record.

This is an integration problem you can solve with APIs and Salesforce Flows. eviCore and Availity both have provider-facing APIs. Varis data can come through your existing payment feeds.

Quick win: Cross-year billing validator. Pre-submission check that flags mixed 2025/2026 claims before they hit Availity. You could ship this in 2 weeks and prevent every one of those denials.

The Money

50K claims/yr. Denial rate 10%→7% = 1,500 fewer denials × ~$200 rework cost = $300K/year saved. Plus: providers stop leaving over billing friction.

VNS's 5-star rating was earned on MLTC relationships built over years. Now VNS is calling new EasyCare members the same way they call MLTC members.

A 45-year-old Medicare HMO member who chose a $25/month plan does not want the same outreach as an 80-year-old with a home health aide.

The blended star rating masks this. By the time segment-specific problems surface, it's 12-18 months too late.

What You Can Do

Segment your engagement by plan type inside Salesforce. You already have the data: plan type, enrollment date, interaction history, portal login frequency, complaint records.

Build Salesforce Flows that route engagement based on segment:

• New D-SNP: Benefits education call within 14 days of enrollment
• New EasyCare: Portal onboarding email + self-service nudge
• Legacy MLTC: Continue high-touch model

This is where your AI PM cert meets production. Interaction patterns as features → churn prediction model → Salesforce surfaces recommendations to care managers.

The Money

MA capitation ~$10K-$15K/member/yr. Losing 5% of 15K = 750 members = $7.5M-$11.25M/yr. Reducing churn by 2 points pays for everything.

Problems #4 & #5
Two cascades compounding silently

The Varis Trap

Varis sends records request. Provider has 30 days. Miss it? Full claim payment reclaimed. Provider did the work. Patient got care. Missed admin deadline = zero pay.

After this happens twice, provider stops accepting VNS members. VNS sees "network attrition" — nobody connects it to Varis.

Action: Build a Varis deadline tracker in Salesforce. Flag claims in post-payment review. Auto-alert provider relations 15 days before deadline. One proactive call saves the relationship.

Advanced: Track providers with 2+ recoupments AND declining claim volume AND no portal logins. That's your "about to leave" signal. Intervene before they do.

Knowledge Walking Out the Door

130 years of institutional knowledge lives in people, not systems. Which providers return calls. Which aides are reliable in which neighborhoods. Which members need their daughter called first.

When veteran staff retire — especially multilingual staff in a 50+ language market — the entire relationship network of a territory vanishes. New hire gets a procedure manual. Not 20 years of relationship intelligence.

Action: Start structured knowledge capture NOW. When a care manager gives notice, run an offboarding protocol: document every provider relationship, member preference, operational workaround, and community contact. Store it in Salesforce as structured notes on the relevant records.

Critical: Map language coverage by geography. If the only Bengali-speaking care manager in the Bronx leaves, you need to know that the day they give notice — not after.

How These Connect

Varis erodes providers → breaks member-provider relationships → drops CAHPS → hits stars → reduces enrollment revenue → constrains hiring → accelerates knowledge drain. It's one system.
