Friction #1

"Prove when you knew"

CMS doesn't just ask: "Is your provider directory accurate?" They ask: "When did you become aware of the inaccuracy, and what did you do about it, and how fast?"

Why You Can't Answer This Today

Provider moves offices January 15
→ Maybe tells VNS. Maybe doesn't.
→ Update comes through credentialing, or a member call, or 90-day outreach
→ Someone updates the Salesforce record on March 3
→ CMS auditor asks: "When did you know?"
→ Salesforce shows the record was updated March 3
→ Cannot prove whether VNS knew Jan 20, Feb 5, or Mar 1
→ Corrective Action Plan issued
→ Directory SUPPRESSED on Medicare Plan Finder
→ Enrollment growth stalls across ALL plans

Salesforce has the current state of every provider record. What it doesn't have is the chain of custody — when each piece of information entered the system, from what source, and what action was taken in response.

The Hidden Cascades

Two frictions that compound silently

The Varis Trap

Varis requests records. Provider has 30 days. Miss it? Full claim reclamation. Provider did the work. Patient got the care. But a missed admin deadline = zero pay.

After this happens twice, the provider stops accepting VNS members. VNS sees "network attrition" and doesn't connect it to Varis.

Graph fix: Provider risk scoring. Flag providers with 2+ recoupments + declining claims + no portal logins. Proactive outreach 15 days before Varis deadline. One call saves the relationship.

Knowledge Walking Out

130 years of institutional knowledge lives in people. Which providers actually return calls. Which aides are reliable in which neighborhoods. Which members need their daughter called first.

When veteran staff retire — especially multilingual staff in a 50+ language market — the entire relationship network of a care territory vanishes.

Graph fix: Institutional knowledge capture. When a care manager announces retirement, generate their full profile — relationships, operational insights, community knowledge — and transfer it.

The Pattern

These aren't separate problems. The Varis trap erodes the provider network, which breaks member-provider relationships, which drops CAHPS scores, which hits star ratings, which reduces enrollment revenue, which constrains workforce investment, which accelerates knowledge drain. It's one system. The graph shows you the whole system.
