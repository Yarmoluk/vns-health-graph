01

"Prove When You Knew" — The CMS Awareness Gap

CMS doesn't just ask "Is your provider directory accurate?" They ask: "When did you become aware that Dr. Smith moved, and what did you do about it, and how fast?"

Most health plans can answer the first question. Almost none can answer the second.

Why VNS Can't Answer It Today

Provider moves offices on January 15
→ Maybe tells VNS. Maybe doesn't.
→ Update comes through credentialing, or a member call, or a 90-day outreach
→ Someone updates the directory on March 3
→ CMS auditor asks: "When did you know?"
→ VNS can show directory updated March 3
→ Cannot prove whether they knew Jan 20, Feb 5, or Mar 1
→ CMS issues Corrective Action Plan
→ Directory SUPPRESSED on Medicare Plan Finder
→ Enrollment growth stalls across ALL plans

The gap isn't data accuracy. It's data provenance — the chain of custody for every piece of provider information.

What We Build: Provider Data Lineage Graph

(:ProviderRecord {npi, name, address, phone})

    timestamp: "2026-01-20T14:32:00Z",
    source: "member_complaint_call",

    response_action: "directory_update_ticket",

    resolution_timestamp: "2026-01-21T09:00:00Z",

  }]->
(:ProviderRecord {updated})

Now when CMS asks "when did you know?" — VNS pulls the graph:
Awareness: Jan 20 at 2:32 PM via member call. Response: Ticket created 38 minutes later. Resolution: Directory updated next business day. SLA: Met.

That's not a compliance answer. That's a compliance weapon.

Why This Is Existential for VNS

VNS is expanding into 25 new counties. Hundreds of new provider records they've never managed before. Without provenance tracking, every new county is a new compliance exposure. With it, expansion becomes a strength.

What We Build: Claims Lifecycle Graph

(:AuthRequest) →[:APPROVED]→ (:Authorization) →[:ATTACHED_TO]→ (:Claim)

The Money

04

The Varis Trap

Varis sends a records request. Provider has 30 days. Miss it? Full claim payment reclaimed as overpayment. The provider did the work. The patient got the care. But a missed deadline means zero pay. After this happens twice, the provider stops accepting VNS members. VNS sees "providers leaving the network" and doesn't connect it to Varis.

What We Build: Provider Risk & Retention Graph

Proactive Varis outreach: 15 days before deadline, VNS — not Varis — calls the provider: "Claim #12345 needs records by March 30. Need help?" One call saves the relationship.

Network attrition prediction: 2+ recoupments AND declining claim volume AND no portal logins in 60 days = about to leave. Intervene before they do.

05

Knowledge Walking Out the Door

130 years of institutional knowledge lives in people. Which providers actually return calls. Which aides are reliable in which neighborhoods. Which members need their daughter called first. When veteran staff retire, that knowledge vanishes. The new hire gets a procedure manual. They don't get 20 years of relationship intelligence.

The 50+ language moat makes it worse. A Mandarin-speaking care manager who retires doesn't just take language — they take the entire relationship network of Mandarin-speaking members and providers in their territory.

What We Build: Institutional Knowledge Graph

Succession planning: When a care manager announces retirement, generate their full knowledge profile — every member relationship, provider connection, operational insight — and transfer it to their successor.

Language coverage mapping: Real-time view of language capability by geography. If the only Bengali-speaking care manager in the Bronx leaves, flag it immediately.
