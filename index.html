Friction #1 — The Compliance Tripwire
"When did you become aware?"

What CMS actually asks during a provider directory audit isn't "Is Dr. Smith's address correct right now?"

CMS asks: "When did you become aware the address was wrong, what did you do about it, and how quickly?"

Most health plans can answer the first question. Almost none can answer the second. Many Corrective Action Plans are issued not because data was wrong, but because the plan couldn't demonstrate its awareness timeline.

Provider moves offices January 15
→ Maybe notifies VNS. Maybe doesn't.
→ Info reaches VNS via member call, credentialing, or 90-day outreach
→ Directory updated March 3
→ CMS asks: "When did you know?"
→ Can show update date. Cannot prove awareness date.
→ Corrective Action Plan issued
→ Directory SUPPRESSED on Medicare Plan Finder
→ Enrollment growth stalls — across ALL plans

The Fix: Provider Data Provenance

Every provider record change needs three things it probably doesn't have today:

1. Source attribution — How did VNS learn about this change? Member call? Credentialing update? 90-day outreach? Provider self-report?

2. Awareness timestamp — Not when the record was updated. When VNS first learned the data was wrong.

3. Response audit trail — What action was taken, by whom, and how quickly? Was the internal SLA met?

CMS asks "when?" — you pull the trail. Awareness: Jan 20, 2:32 PM via member call. Action: ticket created 38 min later. Resolution: next business day. SLA: met.

Why This Is Urgent

VNS is expanding VNS Health Total into 25 additional counties. Hundreds of new provider records never managed before. Without provenance tracking, every new county = new compliance exposure. With it, expansion becomes a compliance strength.

Friction #2 — The Vendor Fragmentation Tax
Four systems. Zero unified view.

A network provider interacting with VNS Health touches four separate vendor systems for a single patient episode:

Submit prior autheviCore portalEvernorth/Cigna

Respond to post-pay auditVarisVaris

Each system has its own login, its own status vocabulary, its own SLAs, and its own support channel. A provider cannot see: "I submitted auth Monday, approved Wednesday, billed Thursday, now flagged for Varis review."

That view doesn't exist. Not for providers. Not inside VNS.

On top of this: the cross-year billing rule (2025 and 2026 services must be on separate claims) is generating predictable, preventable denials right now in Q1 2026. Every provider who bills across years on one claim gets denied.

The Fix: Lifecycle Visibility

VNS needs a unified claim lifecycle view — one place that shows the full journey from auth request through adjudication through post-payment review, regardless of which vendor handled which step.

This means stitching data from eviCore (auth decisions), Availity (claim status), and Varis (audit flags) into a single timeline per provider per member encounter.

Immediate quick win: A cross-year billing validator that catches mixed 2025/2026 claims before submission. This is a rules-based check that could be deployed in weeks and prevent every one of those avoidable denials.

The Cost of Inaction

~50K claims/year. If denial rate drops from industry average 10% to 7% through better visibility and pre-submission validation = 1,500 fewer denials × ~$200 rework cost = $300K/year in avoidable admin cost. Plus: every denial is a provider friction event that pushes toward network departure.

VNS Health's 5-star MLTC rating was earned on relationships built over years. The engagement model — high-touch, phone-based, care-manager-driven — works beautifully for that population.

But 15,000 new members just walked in through D-SNP and EasyCare. A 45-year-old Medicare HMO member who chose a $25/month plan does not want the same outreach as an 80-year-old with a home health aide. One is annoyed. The other depends on it.

The Fix: Segment-Specific Engagement

VNS needs to stratify engagement by plan type and enrollment tenure — not just care complexity.

At minimum, three distinct outreach models:

• New D-SNP: Benefits education within 14 days. Most don't fully understand dual-eligible coverage.
• New EasyCare: Digital onboarding. Portal-first. Self-service-oriented.
• Legacy MLTC: Continue the high-touch model that earned the 5-star rating.

The blended star rating is masking this problem. Overall satisfaction might look fine while D-SNP satisfaction craters. By the time it shows in annual ratings, it's 12-18 months too late to fix.

The Cost of Inaction

MA capitation: ~$10K-$15K per member per year. Losing 5% of 15,000 new members = 750 members = $7.5M-$11.25M annual revenue loss. Reducing churn by even 2 percentage points more than pays for any engagement redesign.

Frictions #4 & #5 — Silent Compounders
Two cascades nobody is connecting

#4: The Post-Payment Audit Trap

Varis conducts post-payment reviews on behalf of VNS. The dynamic:

1. VNS pays a claim
2. Weeks/months later, Varis flags it for clinical validation
3. Varis sends the provider a records request
4. Provider has 30 days to respond
5. Non-response = full claim payment reclaimed as overpayment

The provider did the work. The patient got the care. But a missed admin deadline = zero pay.

After this happens once or twice, the provider stops accepting VNS members. VNS sees "network attrition" in the aggregate data. Nobody connects it to the Varis recoupment that triggered the departure.

Fix: Track which providers are in active Varis review. Proactive outreach from VNS provider relations — not Varis — 15 days before the deadline: "Claim #12345 needs records by March 30. Need help?" One call saves the relationship. Flag providers with 2+ recoupments as at-risk for network exit.

#5: Knowledge Walking Out the Door

VNS has operated for 130 years. The nurses, care managers, and social workers who've been there decades carry knowledge that exists in no system:

• Which providers actually return calls
• Which home health aide agencies are reliable in which neighborhoods
• Which members need their daughter called first before scheduling
• How to navigate a specific county Medicaid office's quirks

When these people retire, that knowledge vanishes. The replacement gets a procedure manual. Not 20 years of relationship intelligence.

The 50+ language moat makes this worse. A Mandarin-speaking care manager who retires doesn't just take language capability — they take the entire relationship network of Mandarin-speaking members and providers in their territory.

Fix: Structured knowledge capture on role transitions. Map language capability by geography. If the only Bengali-speaking care manager in the Bronx gives notice, that needs to be a Day 1 alert — not a discovery after they're gone.

Why These Are Connected

Varis recoupments erode the provider network → member-provider relationships break → CAHPS scores drop → star ratings decline → enrollment revenue falls → workforce hiring constrained → burnout increases → veteran staff retire faster → institutional knowledge lost faster. It's one feedback loop.

Beyond the Five Friction Points
Where connected data changes every value stream

The five problems we walked through are the most urgent. But connecting the data VNS already has unlocks intelligence across the entire health plan operation. Here's where it applies — today.

Claims Operations

Trace a claim from auth request through adjudication through post-pay review — across Availity, eviCore, and Varis — in one view. Detect denial patterns by root cause, not just by code. Catch cross-year billing errors before submission.

Impact: denial rate reduction, admin cost savings, faster provider payment cycles

Provider Network

Model network adequacy by specialty and geography against member population needs — county by county. Track every provider data change with source, timestamp, and response. Score providers by engagement trend, dispute frequency, and recoupment history to predict departures.

Impact: CMS compliance, expansion readiness, network retention

Member Experience

Map the complete care team around each member — PCP, specialist, care manager, aide, pharmacist, social worker. Identify coordination gaps. Segment engagement by plan type, tenure, language, and channel preference. Surface early churn signals from interaction patterns.

Impact: CAHPS scores, star ratings, member retention

Quality & Compliance

Connect VNS's 100 quality measures to find which ones are leading indicators of star rating movement. Map HEDIS gap closure: which members need which interventions, connected to which providers can deliver them. Build audit-ready provenance trails — not just current data accuracy.

Impact: star rating protection, HEDIS performance, audit readiness

Workforce & Operations

Map language capability against member needs by geography. Capture operational intelligence from veteran staff before retirement. Optimize care manager caseloads against member acuity and enrollment growth projections. Alert when a territory loses critical capability.

Impact: burnout prevention, succession planning, language coverage

Population Health

Connect member demographics, zip codes, language, and community resources for social determinant insights. Track care transitions across home care → hospital → hospice → behavioral health. For SelectHealth (HIV+/homeless): map relationships between members, housing programs, and adherence support.

Impact: health equity, outcomes for high-acuity populations
